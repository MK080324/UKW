### 会场一 Section One：

**Committee:** United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

**Topic:** The Situation in Ukraine

**Working Language:** English

**Seat:** 15

**Procedure:** UNSC Provisional Rules of Procedure

**Dais Head:** 周浩浩 Madelyn

---

2013 saw the global order quietly slip off balance. In Ukraine, long-suppressed tensions over identity and deep divisions about the country's foreign-policy direction surfaced all at once. Political polarization and separatist tendencies intensified, putting national stability under severe strain. Meanwhile, the flames of conflict in the Middle East continued to spread. Struggles over power, governance, and security escalated—civilian casualties kept rising, the shadow of chemical weapons controversies still loomed, and extremist forces found space to grow. As multiple crises intertwined, the international situation became increasingly opaque; behind the scenes, competition among major powers was quietly shaping the direction of events.

In this historical simulation, the United Nations Security Council will no longer be a mere observer, but a crucial actor responsible for communication, coordination, and conflict de-escalation. Through multilateral consultation, diplomatic bargaining, and the use of international mechanisms, the Council seeks a possible balance amid regional disorder. Delegates with global vision and a sense of diplomatic responsibility—the Security Council awaits you here to write the choices of 2014. In a moment of turbulence, the hands of history have already turned toward 2014.
